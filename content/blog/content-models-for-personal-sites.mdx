## Why structure matters

Personal sites often start as design exercises and slowly become living archives. The moment that happens, every ad-hoc content decision becomes friction.

## A better baseline

A small content model goes a long way: slugs, excerpts, tags, publication state, and consistent metadata helpers. That gives writing and case studies room to grow without a CMS.

## The payoff

Good structure lowers the cost of publishing. Lower publishing cost means better momentum.
