Problem

Ops teams were juggling spreadsheets, disconnected charts, and chat threads just to understand whether a prompt change was helping or hurting. The product needed a calm, legible interface that could surface anomalies quickly without overwhelming users with raw telemetry.

Process

I mapped the information architecture around three recurring questions: what changed, where risk is rising, and what action is needed next. From there, I built token-driven layouts, tuned chart density for long work sessions, and designed reusable summary modules for experiment reviews.

  • Defined a two-layer dashboard model: high-signal summary first, drill-down analysis second.
  • Created reusable filters, metric cards, and comparison states for multiple data sources.
  • Paired with backend teammates to shape response contracts that supported efficient rendering.
Callout

Design note: the mockup keeps the metadata block separate from the article to match the wireframe contract and give case-study copy a cleaner narrative rhythm.

Outcome

The resulting concept combines strong information hierarchy with technical clarity. Reviewers can scan top-level model health in seconds, jump into drift investigations without losing context, and move from insight to rollout decisions faster.
